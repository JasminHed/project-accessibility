Web Accessibility Quiz

Welcome to our quiz

Our quiz follows accessibility principles to ensure it can be used by everyone. We use clearly group related questions. Labeling for screen reader support. Keyboard navigation is fully supported, allowing users to move through the quiz without a mouse. Overall, the design is simple, clear, and inclusive for all users. This quiz is part of our mission to help more people understand what accessibility really means, and how disability can affect the way people experience the world.

Accessibility Quiz

1. Meet Camilla. Camilla is blind and uses a screen reader to browse the web. Which feature helps Camilla the most when navigating a website?

2. Meet Priya. Priya has ADHD and finds it hard to focus on long blocks of content. What can help her better engage with your app or site?

3. Meet Lisa. Lisa has low vision and prefers high-contrast designs. Which feature supports her needs best?

4. Meet Erik. Erik has dyslexia and finds it challenging to read complex layouts and dense blocks of text. Which design choice would help Erik read and process content more easily?

5. Meet Anders. Anders has hearing loss and relies on visual cues and text to understand content. Which feature would best support his experience on a video-based website?

6. Meet Nour. Nour has limited mobility and uses a keyboard instead of a mouse to navigate digital interfaces. What feature would help Nour the most when using your website?
